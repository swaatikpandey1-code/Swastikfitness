Chapter 2: Acids, Bases and Salts

Introduction

Chemistry is deeply connected with our daily life. The substances we use, the food we eat, and even the medicines we take involve chemical reactions. Acids, Bases and Salts is one of the most important chapters in Class 10 Science because it explains the nature and behavior of these substances.

💡 This chapter is important for board exams and future science studies.

What are Acids?

Acids are substances that produce hydrogen ions (H⁺) when dissolved in water. They are sour in taste and can be corrosive.

  • Turn blue litmus paper red
  • React with metals to produce hydrogen gas
  • Conduct electricity in aqueous solutions

Chemical Reactions of Acids

Acid + Metal → Salt + Hydrogen Gas

Example: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

Acid + Base → Salt + Water (Neutralization)

Bases and Alkalis

Bases are substances that produce hydroxide ions (OH⁻). Alkalis are bases that dissolve in water.

  • Bitter in taste
  • Slippery feel
  • Turn red litmus blue

pH Scale (Important Diagram)

The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14 and is used to measure acidity and basicity.

  • pH less than 7 → Acidic
  • pH equal to 7 → Neutral
  • pH greater than 7 → Basic
⚠️ Lower pH means stronger acid, higher pH means stronger base.

Salts

Salts are formed when acids react with bases in a neutralization reaction. Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Important Chemicals

  • Washing Soda
  • Baking Soda
  • Bleaching Powder
  • Plaster of Paris

Importance in Daily Life

These substances are used in industries, medicines, agriculture and cleaning products. They also help maintain pH balance in our body and environment.
